Edinburgh, the tenth day of September, 1690. FORASMUCH as the Estates of Parliament have granted to Their Majesties a New Supply of Two hun∣dred and sixteen thousand pounds, extending to three Mo∣neths Sess, to be uplifted at Martinmass next, over and above two Moneths Sess, appointed by an Act of the last Session of this Parliament, to be payed at the said Term: Therefore the King and Queens Majesties, and the Estates of Parliament, Do Ordain the Com∣missioners of Supply in the several Shires of this Kingdom, at the next Michaelmass Head-Court, to meet and to Order the said three Moneths Sess, to be Collected with the two Moneths Sess, formerly imposed, and appointed to be payed at Martinmass next; With Power to the saids Commissioners to appoint their subsequent Diets of Meeting, and to take the same course for Raising and Inbringing of this Supply, as for the Supply formerly granted. And Ordains this Presents to be Print∣ed, and Printed Copies thereof to be sent to the Sheriff-Clerks of the se∣veral Shires, to be Intimat by them to the Commissioners of Supply, at the next Michaelmass Head-Court. Extracted forth of the Records of Parliament, by me, THO: BURNET, Cls Reg. God save King VVilliam and Queen Mary. EDINBƲRGH, Printed by the Heir of Andrew Anderson, Printer to Their most Excellent Majesties, 1690.
