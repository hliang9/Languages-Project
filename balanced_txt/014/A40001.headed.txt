Forsomickle as  Collector of the Excise of Aberdene, with consent of the Magistrats of the said Burgh hath given and granted to me  an agreement for the Excise of Brewing of Ale; Beer, and Aquavity, to be ven∣ted, tapped, and sold within my house, from the first of November 1670. to the last of Octo∣ber 1671 years inclusive at  Scots money weekly, during the said space, therefore I the said  and  Cautioner, surety, and full debtor for and with me, bind and obliedges us con∣junctually and severally our Heirs, &c. to pay to him as Collector aforesaid, at the end of every fourth week, foure full weeks Excise, conforme to the said weekly set, untill the end of the said year, and failʒie thereof, the sume of  for ilk moneths deficiency, agreed thereupon with our own consent. And I the said  obliedges me and my foresaids, to relieve my said Cautioner, and his foresaids, of all danger he may incure through my deficiency. Consenting to the re∣gistration hereof in any judicatory books competent within this Kingdome that Letters of Hornig and other Executorials necessar may pass thereupon, on a single cage of six dayes, and to that effect constituts  our lawful Procutors, &c.
