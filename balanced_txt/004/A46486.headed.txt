HIS MAJESTIES LETTER To the Lords Spiritual and Temporal Com∣missioners of Shires and Burroughs assem∣bled, or to be assembled at Edenborough. JAMES R. MY LORDS AND GENTLEMEN, Whereas We have been informed that you the Peers and Representatives of Shires and Burroughs of that our antient Kingdom vvere to meet together at our Good tovvn of Edinborough some time in this instant March by the usurped authority of the Prince of Orange, vve think fit to let you Knovv, that as vve have at all times relyed upon the faith-fulness and affection of you our antient People, so much that in our greatest mis fortunes heretofore vve had recourse to your assistance, and that vvith Good success to our affai∣res, so novv again We require of you to support our Royal Interest, expecting from you vvhat becomes Loyall and Faith∣ful Subjects Generous and Honest men, that you vvill neither suffer your selves to be cajoled nor affrighted into any action misbecoming true hearted Scotsmen, and that to support the honour of your nation you vvill contemn the base example of disloyal men, and eternize your Names by a Loyalty sui∣table to the many Professions you have made to Us, in doing vvhere of you vvill chuse the safest part, since therby you
vvill evite the danger you must needs undergo, the infamy and disgrace you must bring upon your selves in this World, and the condemnation due to the Rebellious in the Next, and you vvill likevvise have the opportunity to secure to your selves and to your Posterity the gracious promises vve have so often made of securing your Religion, Lavvs, Properties, Liberties and Rights, vvich vve are still resolved to perform, assoon as it is possible for Us to meet you safely in a Parlia∣ment of that our antient Kingdom. In the mean time fear no to declare for Us your Lavvful Soveraign, vvho vvill not fail on our part to give you such speedy and povverful assis∣tance as shall not only inable you to defend your selves from any forreign attempt, but put you in a condition to assert our Right against our Enemies, vvho have depressed the same by the blackest of Usurpations, the most unjust as vvell as most unnatural of attempts, vvhich tho almighty God may for a time permit, and let the vvicked prosper, yet the end must bring confusion upon such Workers of Iniquity. We further let you knovv, that vve vvill pardon all such as shall return to their Duty before the last end of this month in∣clusive, and that vve vvill punish vvith the rigour of our Lavvs al such as shall stand out in Rebellion against Us, or our authority. So not doubting that you vvil declare for Us, and suppress vvhatever may oppose our Interest, and that you vvill send some of your Number to Us vvith an account of your diligence, and the posture, of Our affaires there, vve bid you heartily Farevvel. Given on board the St. Michel the 1st of March, and of Our Reign the 5th year. Directed To the Lords Spiritual and Temporal Commissioners of Shires and Burroughs assembled, or to be assembled at our Good Tovvn of Edenborough.
