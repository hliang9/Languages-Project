CATALOGUS Variorum & Insignium LIBRORUM Instructissimae Bibliothecae Clarissimi Doctissimi{que} Viri THOMAE MANTON, S. T. D. Quorum AUCTIO habebitur LONDINI in aedibus Defuncti in Vico Regio prope COVENT-GARDEN, Martis 25. Per Guilielmum Cooper, Bibliopolam. Catalogi gratis Distribuentur ad Insigne Pelicani in Vico vulgo dicto Little-Britain, 1678.
TO THE READER. READER, WE question not but that this manner of Sale by way of Auction is pretty well known to the Learned; Nor can we doubt their Encouragement for the Advantage which They (as well as We) may in time reap thereby; Wherefore we are resolved (Deo volente) to make a fourth triall with the Library of Dr. Tho. Manton, which is not contemptible either for the Value, Conditi∣on or Number, as will appear upon sight thereof, which is free for any Gentlemen that shall please to take that pains: The Conditions and Manner of Proceeding follow: First, That having this Catalogue of the Books, and their Editions under their several Heads and Numbers, it will be more easie for any Person of Quality, Gentlemen or others, to Depute any one to Buy such Book or Books for them as they shall desire, if their occasions will not permit them to be present at the Auction themselves.
Secondly, That he who bids most is the Buyer; And if any manifest Difference shall arise, that then the same Book or Books shall be forthwith exposed again to Sale, and the highest Bidder to have the same. Thirdly, That all the Books according to the Catalogue are (for so much as we know) perfect: But if any of them appear to be otherwise before they be taken away, the Buyer shall have his choice of taking or leaving such Imperfect Book or Books, Fourthly, That the Money for the Book or Books bought, is to be paid by the Buyer within one Month after the Auction or Sale is ended, at the Deceased Doctor's House; where, upon payment thereof, the Book or Books bought, shall be ready to be Delivered within the Month aforesaid: but if any one shall neglect Payment within that Month, then the Mony is to be paid at the Pelican in Little Britain, where the re∣maining Books shall be delivered. Fifthly, That the Auction will begin the 25th. day of March next at the Deceased Doctor's House in King-street in Covent-Garden punctually at Nine of the Clock in the Morning, and Two in the Af∣ternoon;
and this to continue daily until all the Books be sold: Wherefore it is desired, that the Gentlemen, or those Deputed by them, may be there precisely at the Hours appointed, lest they should miss the opportunity of buying those Books which either Themselves or their Friends desire. Lastly, If any Gentleman have a desire to view or see any, or all of these Books in this Catalogue, or to satisfie themselves in the condition and Edi∣tions of any of them, they shall be very welcom to the place afore-named at any time before the day that the Sale begins. This Catalogue was taken by Phil. Briggs, and not by W. Cooper; but afterwards in part methodi∣zed by him: Wherefore he craves your excuse for the mistakes that have hapned; and desires that the Saddle may be laid upon the right Horse.
