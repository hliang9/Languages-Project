LEONARDO DONATO by the Grace of God Duke of Venice, &c. To the most Reuerend Patriarkes, Archbishops, Bishops, of all our Do∣minions of Venice, & to all Vicars Abbots, Priors, Rectors of Parish Churches, and other Prelates Eccle∣siasticall. Health. IT hath come to our know∣ledge that the 17. of Aprill last past by order of the most Holy Father Pope Paule the fift, was published and af∣fixed in Rome a certaine Bull thundred
out against vs, our Senate and Dominion, directed to you, of such Tenor and Con∣tents as therein appeareth. VVhereupon finding our selues bound to preserue in peace and Tranquillitie the State which God hath giuen vs to gouerne: and to maintaine the Authority of a Prince that in temporall affaires acknowledgeth no Su∣perior vnder the diuine Maiestie. VVe by these our publike letters protest before God, and to all the world, that we haue not failed to vse all possible meanes to make his Holinesse capable of our most strong and insoluble Reasons. First by our Ambassador Lieger with his Holinesse: Then by our letters, responsiue to Breises written by him; and finally by a speciall Ambassador sent for this verry purpose. But finding his holines eares shut vnto vs, and seeing the foresaid Bull to haue beene published against the forme of all reason, and contrarie to that which the diuine Scriptures, the doctrine of holy Fathers, and the sacred Canons doe teach vs, in preiudice of the secular Authoritie giuen
vs from God, & of the libertie of our State, with disturbance of the quiet possession, which by Gods grace our faith full subiects vnder our gouernment hold of their goods, honors, and liues, with vniuersall & gree∣uous scandall of all. VVe doubt not a whit to hold the foresaid Bull, not only for vniust and vnlawfull, but also for anulled and of no value, and so without force, fru∣strated, and vnlawfully thundred, De facto nullo Iuris ordine seruato. Wherefore we haue reputed it conuenient to vse against the fame such remedies as our Auncestors and other Soueraigne Princes haue vsed with Popes, that haue extended the power giuen thē by God for edification, beyond their limites. Especially being perswaded that by your & other our Loyall subiects, and by the whole world it shall be so held and reputed. Assuring our selues, that as you haue hitherto, attended to the Cure of soules of our faithfull subiectes, and to the Di∣uine seruice, which through your diligence flourisheth in this our state no lesse thenin
any other so you, will hereafter continue in the selfe same pastorall care, being it our most stedfast Resolution to continue in the holy Catholique and Apostolike faith and in the obseruance of the Holy Romain Church; euen as our Predecessors from the beginning of the foundation of this Citty till this present by the grace of God haue done. And we will that these our Letters for the vnderstanding of all men be affixed in the publike places of this our Citty, and so of all other subiect to our Dominion, Assuring our selues that so manifest a pub∣lication will come to the eares of all such as haue had knowledge of the foresaid Bull; and also to the notice of his Holinesse, whom we beseech our Lord God to in∣spire that he may vnderstand the Nullitie of his Bull & of other his attempts against vs, and that he taking knowledge of the Iustice of our cause, we may be encouraged to obserue our Reuerence to the Holy Sea Apostolike, of the which we and our fore∣fathers, together with this Common∣wealth
haue beene, and will be euer most deuoted. Giuen at our Ducall Pallace the sixth of Maye in the fourth Indiction. 1606. Giacomo Girardo Secretarie. Imprinted by Rampazotto Printer to the Duke.
