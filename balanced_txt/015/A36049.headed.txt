A DIRECTION FOR the English TRAVILLER By which he Shal be inabled to Coast about all England and Wales. And also to know how farre any Market or noteable Towne in any Shire lyeth one from an other and Whether the same be East, West, North, or South from ye Shire Towne As also the distance betweene London and any other Shire or great towne: with the scituation thereof East, West, North, or South from London. By the help also of this worke one may know (in What Parish, Village, or Mansion house soeuer he be in) What Shires, he is to passe thorough & which way he is to trauell▪ till he come to his Journie-End. Jnfalix cuius nulli Sapientia prodest Are to be sold by Thomas Jenner at the South entrance of the Exchange 1643. Jacob van Langeren sculp:
The use of all the insueing Tables. To know the distance betweene any two Cities or Townes in any of theis Tables seeke the places desired in the Ʋpper & side Catalogues of Townes and direct yo eye from either place betweene the lines both from aboue and from the side, and Where the lines meete in square you shall finde the nomber of miles. ffor Example, if you would know how farre Lincolne is from Exier, looke in the card shire Townes and finde Lincolne in the side and carrie yo eye directly from thence betweene the lines vntill it come vnder Exiter, and where they meete in square you shall finde 178. wch is the distance of miles Jf yow finde any towne in the side wich will not extend to make a square wth the desired towne aboue, then looke for the Towne wch yow finde in the side, in the vpper pt. & the vpp. part in the side: For Example Jf you desyre to know the distance betweene Gloucester and Oxford, if you looke Gloucester in the side you cannot bringe Oxford in a square wth Gloucester but if you looke Gloucester aboue and Oxford in the side you shall finde the square and the distance 35 miles and soe of the other ensueing Tables To know whether you, are to trauell East, West, North or South, from the place. where you are to the place whither you intend to goe, & through what Shires you must passe Looke in the small mapp of England placed before the great table of Shire townes and draw a streight line from the next
Shire towne lying in yr way to that Shire towne wch is next the place whither you desyre to goe and you shall imediately see whether your Journie be East, West, North, or South, and what Shires the line brings you through, these Shires you must passe As for Example. Jf you would trauell from London to Yorke, you will perceiue by the mapp that it lyeth North from London then draw a line or carrie yor Eye streight from London to Yorke and you shall finde the way lyeth through Midlesex, Hartford Shire Hunti∣ngton Shire, Northampton Shire, Lincolne Shire, Notinghā Shire, and Yorke Shire, and see for any other place. Againe to know what market townes lie in your way which way soeuer you trauell. When you haue seene in the generall mapp what Shires you are to passe thorough obserue whether you must passe through the midle or the skirts of ech Shire then turne to the tables of those Shires and at the foote of ech of them you shall haue a mapp wherin the townes are signified as they lye in every Shire by the first letter of every towne, and if you must passe through the midle of the Shire then the letters about the midle of the mapp declare the Townes by wch you must goe, but if thorough the skirts of the Shire then the letters in the skirts of the mapp are yor direction. The Market Townes in every table are distinguished from the rest by greate letters, and the letter for ye Shire, townes hath a crosse on the topp to distinguish it from the other
