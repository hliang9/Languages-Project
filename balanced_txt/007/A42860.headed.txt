THat the Reader may the better understand how to make use of the Daily, Weekly, and Monthly Pay-Table, it is necessary to unfold the Officers and Seamens General Pay-Table: Wherein it is to be observed in the first place, that the Captains and Lieutenants are paid by the Day; namely, the Captain in a first Rate has 15s. a day; in a Second Rat 12s in a Third 10s. in  rth 7s. 6d. in a Fifth, 6s. and in a Sixth, 5s. The Lieutenant also has in the irst and Second Rates,  day, and in the Third and Fourth Rates, 2s. 6d. As to other Officers and Seamen, they are paid by the Month; for Example. A Master in a First Rate has 7l. a Month; in a Second, 6l. 6d. in a Third, 4l. 13s. 8d. in a Fourth, 4l. 6s. 2d. in a Fifth, 3l. 17s. and in a Sixth Rate he is Captain and Master. Now suppose you desire to know how much is due to a Gunner of a First Rate, for 5 Months  Weeks and 3 days, then look for Gunner in the first Table under Officers, and opposite to it under 1. which signifies First Rate, you find his pay to be 3. pounds a Month. Then look in the large Table to the right Hand, and under 1. Month then cast your Eye down till you find 3 pound, and opposite to that, and under 5. Month you find that his pay is 15 l. for Five Months. Then to the left Hand, under 3. Weeks, and opposite to 3 l. you find 2l. 5 s. then cast your Eye along in the same line; and under 3 days, you will find 6 s. 5 d. which makes in all 17 l. 11 s. 5 d. which is Gunners pay for so long time and, you must use the same Method for any other, let it be Officer or Seaman. The Figures under N. in the Table of Officers is the Number of Mates, Pilots, Quarter Gunners, and Midshipmen. Note that all the Officers of a Man of War, excepting Mates, has now double the pay mentioned in the Table, the Table being according to the standing Mode of the Navy.
