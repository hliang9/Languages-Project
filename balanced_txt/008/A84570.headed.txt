Anglo-Irish blazon or coat of arms of the Commonwealth AN ACT Enabling such Commissioners of Sewers As Acted on the 19th of April 1653. to Act as Commissioners of Sewers. BE it Enacted by this present Parliament and the Authority thereof, That all and every such person and persons as on the Nineteenth day of April One thousand six hundred fifty and three, did or might act as Commissioners of Sewers in any Counties, Cities, Precincts, Liberties or Places in England, Wales, or Town of Berwick upon Tweed, shall by Force hereof, and by Authority of this present Parliament, be, and shall be adjudged and taken to be Commissioners of Sewers of and in the said respective Counties, Cities, Precincts, Liberties or Places in England, Wales, and Town of Berwick upon Tweed: And so shall continue and be, until there shall be Commissions under the Great Seal of Engâˆ£land, for constituting of Commissioners of Sewers in the said Counties, Cities, Precincts, Liberties or Places, respectively made, and duely issued forth. Wednesday the 18th of May 1659. ORdered by the Parliament, That this Act be forthwith Printed and Published. THO. St. NICHOLAS Clerk of the Parliament. London, Printed by JOHN FIELD, Printer to the Parliament. And are to be sold at the seven Stars in Fleetstreet, over against Dunstans Church, 1659.
