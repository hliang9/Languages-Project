royal blazon or coat of arms C R HONI SOIT QVI MAL Y PENSE DIEV ET MON DROIT ❧ By the King. ❧ A Proclamation for the repressing and punishing of the late Rebellious and Traiterous assemblies in Lambeth, Southwark, and other places adjoyning; and particularly for the apprehension of John Archer, George Seares, William Seltrum, and divers other Rebellious persons. WHereas all those Rebellious and Tumultuous persons, who upon Munday and Thursday nights last, assembled themselves together in a warlike manner in Lambeth, Southwark, and other places, neere unto the City of London, and those who abett, or knowingly conceal, harbour, and relieve them, by the Lawes and Statutes of the Realm are guilty of high Treason; His Majestie out of His Prince∣ly care, to prevent any danger that may happen to His Loving and Loyall Subjects, doth hereby straitly charge and command them, That they and every of them do forthwith use their utmost diligence and endeavour to finde out and apprehend the persons of John Archer of Southwark, Glover, George Seares, Poulterer, and William Seltrum, Shoomaker, who have been principall Actors in the said Traiterous and Rebellious Assem∣blies: And that they further discover and apprehend all other the said wicked and Rebellious persons, and bring them before His Majesties Iustices of Peace, or other Magistrates, whereby they may be delivered over into the hands of Iustice: And that they do not presume to har∣bour, aide, relieve, or conceal them, or any of them, as they will avoide the penalties of the Law in the case of high Treason. And His Majestie doth further straitly charge and command all Housholders, Citizens, and Inhabitants within the Cities of London and Westminster, and the Suburbs thereof, and the Borough of Southwark, and Lambeth, and all other Towns, Villages, and places adjoyning, That they be vigilant and carefull from time to time, to keep their Apprentices and Servants in good government and order within their own houses, and not to suffer them in any night to be, or lodge out of the same, as they will answer the contrary at their utmost perils. And last∣ly, His Majestie doth require and command, as well the Lord Maior of the City of London, and all Lord-Lieutenants, and Deputy-Lieutenants, as all Iustices of Peace, Maiors, She∣riffes, Bayliffs, Constables, Headboroughs, and all other His Officers, Ministers, and Sub∣jects, to be assisting, helping, and aiding in the execution of these His Majesties Royall com∣mands, as they tender the common peace and good of the Kingdom. Given at the Court at Whitehall  this fifteenth day of May, in the sixteenth yeer of His Majesties Reign. ❧ God save the King. ❧ Imprinted at London by ROBERT BARKER, Printer to the Kings most excellent Majestie: And by the Assignes of JOHN BILL. 1640.
