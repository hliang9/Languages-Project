Bibliotheca Bassetiana: OR A CATALOGUE OF Greek, Latin and English BOOKS in most Faculties. BEING THE LIBRARY OF THE Reverend Mr. WILLIAM BASSETT, Late Rector of St. Swithin's, London. Which will be Sold by Auction on Thursday the Fourth of February 1696/7. at Rolls's Auction-House in Petty-Cannon-Alley near the North side of St. Paul's Church. By Edward Millington. Catalogues may be had at Mr. Parker's in the Piazza under the Royal Exchange; Mr. Brown without Temple-Bar; Mr. Castle near Scotland-yard; Mr. Feltham at the foot of the Parliament-Stairs; Mr. Francis Hubbert in Duck-Lane; Mr. Shirley in Oxford, and Mr. Dawson in Cam∣bridge, Booksellers, and at the place of Sale.
Conditions of SALE. I. THE highest Bidder is the Buyer. II. If any Difference arise, the Book shall be re-expos'd. III. If any Book prove Imperfect before it be taken away, the Buyer shall have liberty to refuse it. IV. Any Buyer unknown to the Seller, shall give in his Name, and place of Abode, paying also Five Shillings in the Pound (if demanded) in part for the Books. V. The Money for the Books bought, shall be paid in one Week after the Sale is ended, at the place of Sale, where attendance shall be given for the de∣livery. VI. The Books shall be expos'd to view three days before the Sale. Those Gentlemen who cannot attend, are de∣sir'd to send their Commission to the place of Sale, where they shall be Faithfully executed by NATH. ROLLS▪
